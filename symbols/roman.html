About Roman Numerals

Roman numerals (I, V, X, L, C, D, M) are the numeric system used in ancient Rome. These single-character Unicode Roman numerals are perfectly designed to render properly in digital text, keeping letters grouped together as single symbols (e.g., Ⅷ instead of V I I I). This page allows you to copy single-character versions for perfect formatting.
